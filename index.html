About Us

Econschool is a preparatory program for students aspiring to pursue MA and PhD in Economics at leading institutions in India and abroad — including the Delhi School of Economics (DSE), Indian Statistical Institute (ISI), Jawaharlal Nehru University (JNU), and international universities.

We believe in mathematical rigour and depth over shortcuts. Our focus is on building a strong conceptual foundation in microeconomics, macroeconomics, statistics, and mathematics — the core of any serious graduate economics program.

Econschool also hosts a global community of economics enthusiasts through its YouTube channel (@econ.school), student forum, and workshops on Python, R, and AI tools for economists.

Courses

Our programs prepare students for the most competitive MA and PhD admissions in economics — from DSE and ISI entrance exams to international graduate applications. All courses emphasise rigorous mathematical reasoning and problem-solving over rote preparation.

Free Guided Program

A self-paced, structured program accessible to all students. Covers core topics in microeconomics, macroeconomics, statistics, and mathematics. Supported by the Econschool student forum.

Test Series

A rigorous series of practice tests modelled on DSE, ISI, and JNU entrance patterns. Detailed solutions and performance feedback included.

Classroom Program

Our flagship intensive program covering the full syllabus for MA/PhD entrance preparation. Small batches, live problem-solving sessions, and direct access to Amit Goyal.

Books and Notes

A curated list of textbooks and notes for graduate economics preparation.

  • Microeconomics — Hal Varian, Intermediate Microeconomics; Mas-Colell, Whinston & Green, Microeconomic Theory
  • Game Theory — Osborne & Rubinstein, A Course in Game Theory; Gibbons, Game Theory for Applied Economists
  • Mathematics — Simon & Blume, Mathematics for Economists; Rudin, Principles of Mathematical Analysis
  • Statistics — Hogg, McKean & Craig, Introduction to Mathematical Statistics
  • Macroeconomics — Romer, Advanced Macroeconomics; Blanchard & Fischer, Lectures on Macroeconomics
  • Econ Artbook — A 100-page collection of mathematical postcards created using TikZ and Mathcha by Amit Goyal

References and Reading Lists

Landmark papers and reading lists for students interested in going beyond the textbook.

  • Akerlof, G. (1970) — The Market for Lemons — information asymmetry and adverse selection
  • Spence, M. (1973) — Job Market Signaling — signaling under asymmetric information
  • Diamond, P. & Dybvig, P. (1983) — Bank Runs, Deposit Insurance, and Liquidity
  • Milgrom, P. & Roberts, J. (1982) — Predation, Reputation, and Entry Deterrence
  • Nash, J. (1950) — Equilibrium Points in N-Person Games
