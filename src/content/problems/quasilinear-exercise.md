## Exercise

A consumer has utility $u(x, y) = \ln x + y$, prices $p_x, p_y > 0$, and income
$m > 0$. The quantity $y$ may be interpreted as money spent on all other goods, so
$y \geq 0$ is required but $y$ need not be an integer.

1. Derive the demand functions for $x$ and $y$, assuming an interior solution.
2. For what values of $m$ does the interior solution fail? Derive the demand
   functions in that case.
3. Show that, at an interior solution, the demand for $x$ is independent of income.
   Why is this property useful in applied welfare analysis?

*Hint for part 2:* check when your expression for $y^*$ from part 1 turns negative,
and think about what the consumer does with their whole budget then.
