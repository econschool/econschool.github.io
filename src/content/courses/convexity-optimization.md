Convexity is the structure that makes optimization in economics work. This
course builds the ideas in order — convex sets, concave and quasi-concave
functions, then unconstrained and constrained optimization through the
Lagrangian method and the Kuhn–Tucker conditions — with the economic use kept
in view throughout. Calculus here is a tool we assume rather than the subject;
the subject is convex structure and what it buys us.
