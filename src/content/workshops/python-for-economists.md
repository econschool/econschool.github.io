Python has become the default computational language in economics — for data work,
for simulation, and increasingly for research itself. This workshop takes you from
zero to writing short programs that do real economic work.

## What we cover

1. **The basics, properly.** Variables, functions, lists, and loops — taught through
   economic examples, not abstract exercises.
2. **NumPy and Matplotlib.** Arrays, vectorised computation, and plotting — enough to
   draw a demand curve from a utility function rather than from memory.
3. **A small project.** We end by simulating a simple exchange economy and watching
   prices converge to equilibrium.

## Who it is for

Students preparing for graduate study who want computation to be a strength rather
than a gap. If you can use a spreadsheet, you are ready.

## Format

Live online sessions with exercises between meetings. You write code from the first
hour. All materials remain available afterwards.
