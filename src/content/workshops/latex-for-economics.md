Every graduate program expects problem sets and papers in LaTeX. Learning it early
removes a real source of friction later — and well-set mathematics is simply easier
to read and to check.

## What we cover

1. **A working document from scratch.** Structure, packages, and a clean preamble
   you can reuse for every problem set.
2. **Mathematics.** Equations, alignment, matrices, and the conventions of
   mathematical writing in economics.
3. **Presentations and diagrams.** A first beamer deck, and an introduction to TikZ
   for economic diagrams — budget sets, indifference curves, game trees.

## Who it is for

Anyone who will write mathematics this year. No prior LaTeX experience assumed.
